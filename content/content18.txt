Frequently Asked Questions: Paying for Credit Repair Help 
Q: Is it worth paying for credit repair help if I only have one default? 
A: Usually yes. One default can block all finance applications. The cost of professional removal (typically $500-$730) is recovered quickly through better interest rates. Most clients save more in their first month than the entire repair cost. 
Q: What if I pay for credit repair help but nothing gets removed? 
A: Reputable companies operate on No Win No Fee basis. You only pay success fees when items are actually removed. The admin fee covers professional assessment and recommendations even if removal isn't possible (rare with proper initial evaluation). 
Q: Can I negotiate lower fees when paying for credit repair help? 
A: Professional companies have standard pricing for good reason - they know the value they deliver. Be wary of companies offering significant discounts, as this often indicates corners being cut in service quality. 
Q: How do I know if paying for credit repair help is better than waiting 5 years? 
A: Calculate the opportunity cost. If waiting 5 years costs you $50,000+ in higher interest rates, delayed purchases, or missed opportunities, paying $500-$1,200 for immediate resolution makes financial sense.
Q: What's included when I pay for credit repair help? 
A: With reputable companies: credit file analysis, legal strategy development, dispute letter writing, creditor negotiations, compliance escalations, ongoing case management, and complete removal or money back protection. 
Q: Should I pay for credit repair help from overseas companies? 
A: No. Australian credit law requires local expertise and ASIC licensing. Overseas companies can't provide proper consumer protection and often make situations worse with poor understanding of Australian regulations.
